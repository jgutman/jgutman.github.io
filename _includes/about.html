About

I'm currently a graduate student at the NYU Center for Data Science, where I work as a teaching assistant, researcher, and statistical consultant, completing my M.S. in Data Science in 2016. My background is in educational measurement, cognitive science, and machine learning, with a strongly computational and Bayesian bent. I earned my B.S. in Brain & Cognitive Sciences from the University of Rochester in 2012.
Substantively, my primary research interests lie in developing a framework for the assessment of collaborative problem-solving within educational technology platforms, understanding the relationship between metacognitive and cognitive factors in learning growth curve rates, and causal inference for policy interventions in education and racial justice issues.
